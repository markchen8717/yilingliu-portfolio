Risk of Beauty

The moment you stand in the spotlight, you will draw all manner of people to you.
2025. Porcelain, wire, elastic string, glass bead, wig, fabric, acrylic board, fishing wire, mirror, single reflective film, light, electric fan. 24×24×24 inch.
Beauty is a double-edged sword. Growing up with an aesthetic education, I’ve always been fascinated with pursuing beauty, but I never knew what beauty could bring me. The “Risk of Beauty” is the second in my “Exploration of Beauty” trilogy. The first one is “Pursuit of Beauty,” where I fanatically chase the fashion trend, starve myself, simply to fit in the morbid aesthetic standard of slim bodies. The second stage is where I finally become beautiful enough to be the center of attention. This beauty I’ve paid so much for was bringing me more than I expected: it attracts people of all kinds. Sometimes I feel myself falling into an abyss, while there are numerous eyes staring at me, but when I try to look back at them, they tend to disappear. The third stage would be “Manipulation of Beauty”, which is an ideal stage I describe for myself: appropriately make use of the beauty I possess, but smartly avoid getting harmed in any way. While I gradually realize more about the truths of the world and how beauty is perceived and functioned, I’m still exploring the exact methodology to manipulate beauty.
